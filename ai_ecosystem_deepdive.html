01
Equipment
Lithography, etch, deposition & metrology tools
The most concentrated layer — a handful of equipment makers control the physical tools needed to fabricate any chip. EUV lithography (ASML) is the single biggest bottleneck.
→ wafers & process recipes flow down
02
Foundry
Chip manufacturing at leading nodes
TSMC dominates advanced AI chip production (3nm/5nm). Intel Foundry competes with 18A process. Every major AI chip is fabricated here before packaging.
→ bare dies flow to packaging
03
Memory
HBM, DRAM & storage for AI workloads
HBM3E is the primary AI memory bottleneck — SK Hynix sold out its entire 2026 HBM supply. Micron is ramping fast. Every GPU training cluster needs massive memory bandwidth.
→ memory stacked onto compute dies
04
Packaging
Advanced packaging & semiconductor test
CoWoS packaging (TSMC) and OSAT players integrate GPU dies with HBM. Amkor and ASE are critical for volume production. TSMC CoWoS is sold out through 2026.
→ packaged chips assembled into systems
05
AI Chips
GPUs, custom ASICs & AI accelerators
NVIDIA GPUs dominate training. 2026 is the year of custom ASICs — Broadcom co-designs 70% of hyperscaler custom chips. Marvell builds ASICs for Amazon/Microsoft. AMD MI350 gaining share.
→ chips connected via high-speed networks
06
Networking
Interconnects, optical & switching
Networking is the emerging bottleneck after compute. 100,000-GPU clusters need ultra-low latency optical interconnects. AAOI transceivers, Arista switches, and Amphenol connectors are all critical.
→ systems need power & thermal management
07
Power & Cooling
Energy supply, UPS & liquid cooling
Blackwell GPU racks draw 120kW each — 5–10x prior generation. Liquid cooling is now mandatory. Nuclear & gas power are being contracted directly by hyperscalers (Microsoft/Three Mile Island).
→ infrastructure hosted in data centers
08
Cloud & Data Centers
Hyperscalers, colocation & edge
AWS, Azure, Google Cloud and Oracle are spending $660–690B in capex in 2026 — 75% on AI infrastructure. They also build their own custom silicon (TPU, Trainium, Maia) competing with NVIDIA.
→ compute rented by model labs
09
Foundation Models
LLMs, multimodal & specialized AI
GPT-5, Gemini 2, Claude 4, LLaMA 3 — these are the intelligence layer that enterprises build on. By 2026, 60%+ of large organizations have based AI strategies on foundation models. APIs and fine-tuning enable the app layer.
→ models deployed via APIs to applications
10
Dev Tools & MLOps
Orchestration, data & fine-tuning platforms
The middleware connecting model APIs to production apps. Data labeling (Scale AI), model tracking (W&B), vector databases (Pinecone), and orchestration (LangChain) form the invisible glue layer.
→ powering real-world AI applications
11
Applications
Downstream AI products across all verticals
The value capture layer — enterprise SaaS, autonomous systems, consumer AI assistants, and vertical AI. By 2026, 80%+ of enterprise apps embed AI (Gartner). This is where AI revenue becomes visible to end users.
